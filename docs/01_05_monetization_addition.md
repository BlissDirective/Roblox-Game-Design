### CPM creator marketplaces (the 2026 organic-paid hybrid)

Starting in 2025, a new category of marketing platform emerged for Roblox: **third-party CPM marketplaces** that pay short-form creators per verified view to promote your game. They sit between flat-fee influencer drops and pure organic — you set a budget and a CPM, the platform pulls a creator pool, those creators make TikToks/Shorts/Reels of your game on their own schedule, and they get paid per 1,000 verified views their post generates.

**This is not a Roblox first-party product.** Roblox's "Creator Rewards" program (formerly Engagement-Based Payouts) is a different thing — that pays *you* for retaining players. CPM marketplaces are external vendors. Examples in the space include BLOXG, Adaptive AI / Roblox Forge, and others; the category is new and shifting fast, so verify any vendor's terms before committing budget.

**Why the math is interesting.** Reported CPMs in this space sit roughly between **$0.50 and $1.50 per 1,000 views**, well below TikTok's own paid-ad CPM (~$5–$10) and Meta's (~$10+). The output is also organic-feeling — the creators are real people making content the way they normally would, sitting next to other organic videos in the feed, rather than a clearly-marked ad. The trade-off: you control the *brief* but not the individual creative, and quality varies.

**The funnel math.**

```
$1,000 budget ÷ $0.75 CPM = ~1.3M views
1.3M views × 0.5% click-to-install = ~6,500 new game visits
6,500 visits × ~25% D1 retention = ~1,600 retained players on day 1
Of those, monetization stack from earlier in this doc applies.
```

The **0.5% views-to-install rate is your single most important metric** to watch. If your hook doesn't pull the right viewer, the cheapest CPM in the world won't matter. Rewrite the campaign brief before scaling the budget.

**When this works:**

- Your game has a clear visual hook in the first 2 seconds (the "spectacle moment" from `04_VIRAL_MECHANICS.md`).
- Your D1 retention is already above ~25% (you're not paying to acquire players who bounce).
- Your monetization is already converting at >2% (you can recoup CPM cost from the players who stick).

**When it doesn't:**

- D1 retention below 15%. You'll burn $1k for nothing.
- A boring or non-clippable game. Creators won't be able to make a hook that performs, no matter the CPM.
- Pre-launch. Wait until you have data to know your hook works organically — then scale with paid creators.

**How it stacks in the funnel.** A useful mental model:

1. **Free organic** (your own TikToks, Discord, in-game word-of-mouth) → finds your initial 50–500 D1 testers and confirms the hook works.
2. **CPM marketplace** → scales the hook that's already working, cheaply, to 5K–50K new players.
3. **Roblox Sponsored Games** → only after CPM is converting, to ride the algorithm signal that comes with paid acquisition.
4. **Flat-fee influencer drops** → reserve for game-changing moments (major update, season launch, event tie-in). The flat fee detaches the creator's pay from your performance, so the bar is "can this single creator move the needle in a way 50 small creators can't."

**Mistake to avoid:** paying flat fees as your *first* marketing motion. CPM ties the creator's paycheck to your performance; flat fees tie nothing to anything. Use flat fees only when you have a specific reason to need a specific creator.

**Reality check on the data.** Vendor case studies in this space (e.g., reports of $4K spend → 7.5M views) are real but cherry-picked. They're the campaigns where the hook landed, not the median. Plan around a $0.75–$1.00 effective CPM, not the headline rate, and assume your first campaign will perform 30–50% worse than your fifth as you learn what creative works for your game.
